Theory



What is a Classifier?

A classifier is a model, that is used to differentiate between objects, based on certain characteristics.



What is a Naive Bayes' Classifier?

  • This classification algorithm assumes independence among predictors, i.e. the presence of any characteristic is independent of the presence/absence of any other characteristics.
  • It is a linear classifier.
  • It is the most simple and fast classification algorithm.
  • The crux of this classifier is based on Bayeâ€™s Theorem.
  • It is a probabilistic classifier, which means it predicts on the basis of the probability of an object.



Where is Naive Bayes' Classifier used?

  • Face Recognition, it is used to identify the faces and it's features.
  • Spam Filtering, it can be used to distinguish an email as spam or not.
  • Weather Prediction, it is used to predict the weather.
  • Sentiment Analysis, it is used to identify a customer's positive and negative sentiments.
  • Recommendation Systems, along with data mining techniques it can identify whether a resource will be liked by the user or not.

Some Terminologies

We'll explain the terminologies considering the spam filtering example. Suppose, we have incomings emails and we want to classify them as spam or not spam. For this purpose we have considered attributes as words and their respective freqeuncies to be considered to predict whether an email is a spam or ham.



  • Posterior Probability

    It can be defined as the probability, that a particular object belongs to class i, provided that it has certain features. According to the formula it is the Probability of A being true, given that B is true.

    For Example, the probability the incoming email is spam or not based on the words we have in the email, with their respective count, is the posterior probability.

    P (spam | xi)


  • Prior Probability

    This is the probability that the event occurs. This is calculated from the previous data collected. According to the formula it is the, Probability of A.

    For Example, the probability that the incoming mail is a spam.

    P (spam)


  • Conditional Probability

    This the probability that the object belonged to category i, when the conditions were fulfilled. According to the formula, it is Probability of B being true, given that A is true.

    For Example, the probability from the data, that the given mail had the xi features and it was a spam.

    P (xi | spam)


  • Evidence

    This the probability that the conditions were fulfilled independent of the class. According to the formula, it is Probability of B being true.

    For Example, the probability from the data, that the given mail contains the specified words and frequencies.

    P (xi)
